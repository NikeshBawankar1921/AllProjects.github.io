Line Maze Solver

A Line Maze Solver is a program designed to autonomously navigate through a maze composed of lines and open spaces. Using algorithms like depth-first search or breadth-first search, it efficiently determines the optimal path by following the lines from the maze's start to its end. The solver provides a sequence of movements or directions, demonstrating its ability to navigate through the maze intelligently.

bidirectional visitor counter

A bidirectional visitor counter utilizes two infrared sensors and an 8051 microcontroller to accurately count incoming and outgoing visitors. With the infrared sensors strategically placed, the system can distinguish the direction of movement, providing an efficient solution for tracking foot traffic in both directions. This intelligent design makes it suitable for various applications, such as entrance monitoring in buildings or event venues.

Faster Finger First circuit

The Faster Finger First circuit, employing an Arduino Uno, four switches, and a 16x2 LED display, enables rapid response challenges. Participants compete to press their switches first, with the Arduino quickly detecting and displaying the winning participant on the LED screen. This interactive circuit is ideal for quiz shows or competitive events, enhancing engagement with its swift and accurate response system.

Dot Matrix Display

An Arduino Nano paired with 5mm LEDs and shift registers IC forms an efficient LED display system. By utilizing shift registers, this setup enables the control of multiple LEDs with minimal pins, making it suitable for intricate lighting projects. The Arduino Nano's compact size and the shift register's ability to expand output capacity make this combination versatile for various creative applications.

Wireless Doorbell

A wireless doorbell employing RF modules provides a convenient and cable-free solution for door signaling. Utilizing RF communication, this system allows users to install the doorbell button and receiver at separate locations within a specified range, enhancing flexibility and ease of use. The RF-based wireless doorbell ensures reliable and instantaneous notification for visitors without the need for traditional wired connections.

DIY PCB Etching

A PCB etching solution using hydrochloric acid (HCl) and hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) creates an effective and cost-efficient method for circuit board fabrication. The mixture of HCl and H2O2 acts as a powerful etchant, selectively removing unwanted copper from the PCB surface during the etching process. This DIY etching solution offers a straightforward approach to creating custom printed circuit boards for electronic projects.
